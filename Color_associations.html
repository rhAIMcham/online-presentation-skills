Colour Psychological Effect Best Used For
Blue Promotes trust, calm and confidence. Often associated with reliability and intelligence. Corporate or formal presentations, topics involving data, strategy or leadership.
Green Symbolises growth, balance and renewal. Has a calming and reassuring effect. Environmental themes, health and wellbeing, change management or personal development topics.
Red Represents passion, energy and urgency. Grabs attention quickly but can be overpowering if overused. Key points, calls to action or moments that require emotional emphasis.
Yellow Reflects optimism, warmth and creativity. Too much can cause visual fatigue. Highlighting key ideas, creative sessions or motivational presentations.
Orange Combines energy and friendliness. Invokes enthusiasm and approachability. Informal team updates, product launches or engaging storytelling.
Purple Suggests creativity, wisdom and luxury. Creates a sense of sophistication. Inspirational or visionary presentations.
Black/Grey Conveys authority, formality and professionalism. Too much can seem heavy or distant. Formal topics, contrast backgrounds or minimalistic design themes.
White Represents clarity, simplicity and space. Helps balance other strong colours. Backgrounds or minimalist layouts where focus should be on content.
